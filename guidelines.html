Community Guidelines

The Udacity Community aims to foster collaboration amongst its participants. In order to do so, we ask that hosts and members abide by some basic guidelines.

Be respectful

Udacity Communities are diverse and with diversity comes various opinions. Respect other’s opinions and ways. Everyone in Udacity Community should take the responsibility of not indulging in any sort of scuffle. Additionally work towards dissolving arguments if any.

Be collaborative

Together we can do so much more. Collaborate and work towards each other’s success. Let’s learn collaboratively.

Foster meaningful and genuine interactions

Ask the right questions and please don’t spam.

Take responsibility and lead by example

This is your community. Be a part of discussions, show-up for events, share your feedback. Be a leader not a boss.

Follow the Law

Doing things with proper syntax and semantics produces less errors. Let’s stick to that practice in the community also.

FAQs

  • Do I have to be a Nanodegree student/graduate to lead a chapter?

    The Udacity Community aims to foster collaboration amongst its participants. In order to do so, we ask that hosts and members abide by some basic guidelines.

  • Do I have to be a Nanodegree student/graduate to participate in events?

    Yes and No. Normally the meetups are open to all, regardless of you being a Nanodegree student or not. However, events like study groups, ND hackathon, ND Q&A which are more concentrated for Nanodegree students will cater only to them.

  • I am not enrolled in a Nanodegree, but can I volunteer in the events?

    Yes, you can! If the organizer is happy to take help, you surely can take up tasks available and make the community management easy.
